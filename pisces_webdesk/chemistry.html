Write short notes on carbocation, carbanion and carbon free radical.

Ans. Carbocation, carbanion and carbon free radical are reaction intermediates.
Carbocation is a positively charged species. It has 6 paired electrons. It has vacant p-orbital.
The carbon carrying positive charge is sp2-hybridised and has planar geometry.
The carbocation has a flat structure having three covalent bonds in one plane with a bond angle of 120° between them.

Carbanion is a negatively charged species.
It has 8 paired electrons i.e., has octet of electrons. It is sp3- hybridised molecule having pyramidal geometry.
The three sp3 hybrid orbitals are used in making three σ bonds with other atoms. The fourth sp3-hybridised orbital contains a lone
pair of electrons.

Carbon free radical is species having trivalent carbon atom bearing no charge.
It has odd or unpaired electron thus making the species paramagnetic.
The carbon centre contains 7 electrons.
It has planar geometry in which the three coplanar hybrid orbitals are involved in the formation of three single bonds with other atoms.
The bond angle around the carbon bearing odd electron is approximately 120° each, and the odd electron remains in the hybridised
p-orbital present in a plane at right angle to the plane of hybrid orbitals

Q. Arrange the following in the decreasing order of acidity:



I. p-methoxy benzoic acid II. o-methoxy benzoic acid III. m-methoxy benzoic acid IV. Benzoic acid

Ans. o-methoxy benzoic acid>m-methoxy benzoic acid>benzoic acid>p-methoxy benzoic acid.
The -OCH3 group shows +M and -I effect.
In p-methoxy benzoic acid the -OCH3 group at the para position shows +M and -I effect.
As the mesomeric effect is stronger than the inductive effect the -OCH3 group predominantly shows +M effect thereby act
as electron donating group.
The electron-donating tendency destabilises the negative charge developed after the release of the proton.
The o-methoxy benzoic acid is most acidic among all because of the ortho effect(any group at the ortho-position
of the benzoic acid increases its acidity) present in the molecule.
In m-methoxy benzoic acid, the -OCH3 group at the meta-position only shows -I effect.
Thereby, stabilises the negative charge developed after the release of the proton.
